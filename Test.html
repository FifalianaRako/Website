More than a game a battle of wits that has not changed in centuries.

Born in the 6th-century in India as Chaturanga, chess began as a literal war map. It traveled through Persian palaces and Silk Road trade routes before landing in Europe. What started as a training tool for generals became the ultimate test of human logic.

Why You Should Care ?

1/ Infinite Variety

"Chess is the gymnasium of the mind."

-Blaise Pascal

Whether you are a grandmaster or a first-timer, every game starts with the same 20 opening moves, but ends in a story that has never been told before since there are more possible chess games than there are atoms in the observable universe.You will never play the same game twice.

2/ Mental Gym

Studies show that playing chess can physically alter your brain structure, improving memory and creative problem-solving.
